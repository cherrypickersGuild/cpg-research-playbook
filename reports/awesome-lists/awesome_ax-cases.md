## Synthesis

The strongest available source for "ax-cases" turned out not to be an awesome-list of demo repos but `themanojdesai/genai-llm-ml-case-studies` (1,620 stars), a purpose-built index of 500+ real, named-company engineering blog posts about production LLM/GenAI deployments — the seed list (`awesome-llm-apps`) was verified and rejected because it curates runnable open-source agent templates, not company transformation narratives. Across the 40 entries reviewed, a handful of workflow patterns recur independently across many companies: (1) natural-language-to-SQL agents replacing analyst query-writing (Uber QueryGPT, Swiggy Hermes, Pinterest, LinkedIn), (2) internal support/on-call copilots retrieving from internal docs (Uber Genie, DoorDash Dasher support, MongoDB docs chatbot, Thomson Reuters), and (3) human-agent-augmentation copilots in live chat/sales (Wayfair Agent Co-Pilot). The single best-evidenced, fully-quantified case studies obtained by fetching the original articles (not just the repo stub) are **Uber's Genie** (70,000+ questions answered, 13,000 engineering hours saved, 48.9% helpfulness), **Wayfair's Agent Co-Pilot** (10% reduction in average handle time), and **GitHub Copilot** (up to 55% faster coding in a blind study, 74% less developer frustration) — these three are recommended as anchor examples for an AX curriculum since they combine a named company, an explicit before/after workflow description, and a hard KPI. The remaining ~34 entries are real, named, verifiable deployments but the repo's own subpages are thin stubs (TL;DR + tags + date) pointing to external blogs that were not all independently re-fetched, so most rows here document *that* a transformation happened and roughly *what kind*, without an independently-verified quantitative KPI.
